Bio:

I've always had a passion for technology and gadgets since a very young age. When we were young, my brother and I received lots of electronic toys for christmas. From radio controlled cars to controllable robots, year after year my brother and I would eventually break the toys our parents got us. Now, I'm sure our parents didn't know this at the time, but we weren't necessarily being "boys with toys", breaking our toys just for the hell of it...I desperately wanted to know how the thing worked. After figuring out that it was actually little motors and gears that made the toys move, I eventually built my own little 3-wheeled go cart toy out of spare parts we had from breaking apart our other toys. Following in my footsteps my little brother built one of his own and we spent a few long, hot and boring summers racing our go carts against each other. That go cart was my favorite toy that I played with more than even my store-bought toys, constantly upgrading and improving it along the way. I think I loved that go cart so much because it was something I had made with my own hands and imagination, and nobody told me to do it. It was then that I knew I wanted to build things for a living, as I was engineering before I even knew what the word meant. From there, I learned programming and the rest is as they say history...
